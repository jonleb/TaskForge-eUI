        <p class="eui-u-text-paragraph">
            <strong>Patterns</strong> can be defined as a toolkit of typical solutions and best practices to solve common problems in Software Design.
            Each pattern is like a blueprint that can be customized to solve a particular design problem.
        </p>
        <p class="eui-u-text-paragraph">
            The target audience goes from Designers & Developers who will find a lot of usefull information regarding recommended usage and UX Guidelines and also Analysts and Managers
            to help them during conceptual phases of a project.
        </p>        

        <p class="eui-u-text-paragraph">
            <strong>Design Patterns</strong> are reusable combinations of eUI components (compositions) aimed to solve common user interface problems
            in software design and to enhance the overall user experience.
            <br>
            It is a description or template for <strong>how to solve a problem</strong> that can be used in many different situations.
        </p>
        <p class="eui-u-text-paragraph">
            These best practice solutions help users achieve their goals and help ensure consistency across experiences.
        </p>

        <p class="eui-u-text-paragraph">They also define a <strong>common language</strong> and <strong>common layout</strong> that helps teams communicate more efficiently and,

        <div class="eui-u-text-h4 eui-u-mt-l">

        </div>

                eUI Desktop patterns are a collection of business agnostic patterns &mdash; <strong>a combination of eUI components</strong> (compositions) &mdash;
                aimed to solve common user interface problems and to enhance the overall user experience that helps to create consistent user interfaces.

                They are part of the <strong>eUI Design System</strong> made with <a class="eui-u-text-link-external" href="https://www.figma.com/&#64;eceui" target="_blank">Figma design platform</a>
                and based on <strong>a collection of best practices</strong> gathered from various mid to large scale projects over past few years.
        </p>
