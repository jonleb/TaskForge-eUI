# EC actions fostering digital inclusion and accessibility

##	Web Accessibility Directive(WAD) for public sector
(https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/LSU/?uri=CELEX:32016L2102)
* Websites of the public sector
* Mobile Apps of the public sector
* [Harmonised European standard]( https://www.etsi.org/deliver/etsi_en/301500_301599/301549/03.02.01_60/en_301549v030201p.pdf ) to make it easier for business and member states that they are following the a11y standards and show that they are compliant.
	
## European Accessibility Act
(https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/LSU/?uri=CELEX:32019L0882)
* refers to the private sector
* accessible computers and operating systems;
* extends the a11y requirements to key ICT products and services, from checking your bank account to buying concert tickets, e-books et al.

## Digital Services Act
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/digital-services-act-package)
* All digital services
* Codes of conduct for a11y to promote full participation by improving access to online services

##	Marrakesh Directive and regulation
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/marrakesh-treaty)
* Print works, including e-books, in formats adapted for persons who are blind, visually impaired or those who have reading difficulties (such as dyslexia).

## Audiovisual Media Services directive
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/audiovisual-and-media-services)
* Sign language / subtitling /audio descriptions for both television broadcasting and video on demand

## European Electronic Communications Code
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/eu-electronic-communications-code)
* equivalent services to users with disabilities;
* alternative channels to reach emergency services (such as real time text, SMS or emergency applications) should be available.

## Roaming Regulation
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/roaming)
* facilitate access to emergency services for end-users with disabilities

## eIDAS Regulation
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/eidas-regulation)
* accessible trust services and end-user products (e.g. electronic signature).

##	Artificial Intelligence Act
(https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/european-approach-artificial-intelligence)
* codes of conduct on accessibility
* Protection of fundamental rights, including for persons with a disability in order not to extend existing discrimination

## Strategy for the rights of persons with disabilities 2021-2030
(https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=1484&langId=en)
Flagship initiatives:
* AccessibleEU: a knowledge base providing information and good practices on accessibility across sectors (by end 2022)
* European Disability Card: the European Commission will propose a European Disability Card that would apply to all EU countries. The card will make it easier for persons with disabilities to get the proper support when they travel or move to another country in the European Union. (by end 2023)
* Guidance recommending improvements on independent living and inclusion in the community. This will contribute to enabling persons with disabilities to live in accessible, supported housing in the community or to continue living at home (2023).
* A framework for social services of excellence for persons with disabilities (2024)
* A package to improve labour market outcomes of persons with disabilities (to be launched second half of 2022)
* Disability Platform: The Disability Platform brings together national authorities responsible for implementation of the Convention, organisations of persons with disabilities and the Commission. It supports the implementation of the strategy and enhances cooperation and exchange on implementing the Convention.
* Renewed HR strategy for the European Commission, including actions to promote diversity and inclusion of persons with disabilities.

## Web accessibility action plan
* Applies to EU institutions
* Adopted in September 2022
* Multiannual (2022-2025)
* Available on Europa Web Guide [here](https://www.eud.eu/web-accessibility-action-plan%EF%BF%BC/)
* The aim is to ensure compliance of the institutions' docs, websites and all the material of the europa domain with the applicable a11y standards.
* It has six action areas with specific timeline and outcomes:
    - Rules and guidelines:
        - Requirements
        - Checklists
        - Testing methods
        - Accessibility reporting
        - Templates
    * Accessibility statements, feedback mechanism
        - Inform the users of any inaccessible content (appearing as little mushrooms popping up)
        - Published on core sites: i.e. commission.europa.eu, european-union.europa.eu, clearing listing the a11y issues
        - Aim for the end of 2023 is to populate all the Europa domain with the remaining accessibility statements
    - Compliance monitoring
        - Continuous monitoring of Commission's web presence for compliance with the web accesibility directive and with WCAG 2.1 AA.
        - Combination of automated and manual checks
        - Results shared with site owners
        - Individual support offered by accessibility experts
    * Awareness raising and internal communication
        - Internal communication plan for 2023
        - Monthly accessibility tips
        - Info sessions and webinars
    - Training
        - Basics of web accessibility
        - Tailor-made trainings for different profiles to be organised
    * Organisational coordination
        - Web Accessibility Task Force (6 DGs)
        - Accessibility coordinators in each Directorate General
